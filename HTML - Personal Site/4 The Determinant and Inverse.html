The Determinant

Every square matrix has a quality associated with it, called the determinant. It can be used to solve sets of equations. The determinant of a matrix can be denoted in three ways - det($A$), det $A$, or $\begin{vmatrix}A\end{vmatrix}$.

Calculating the Determinant of a 2 x 2 Matrix


det $\begin{bmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{bmatrix} =\begin{vmatrix}a & b\\c & d\end{vmatrix} = ad-bc$

As an example:    det$\begin{bmatrix}2&4\\3&5\end{bmatrix} =\begin{vmatrix}2 & 4\\3 & 5\end{vmatrix} = 10-12 = -2$

Calculating the Determinant of a 3 x 3 Matrix

det $\begin{bmatrix}a&b&c\\d&e&f\\g&h&i\end{bmatrix} =\begin{vmatrix}a&b&c\\d&e&f\\g&h&i\end{vmatrix} = a\begin{vmatrix}e&f\\h&i\end{vmatrix} -b\begin{vmatrix}d&f\\g&i\end{vmatrix}+c\begin{vmatrix}d&e\\g&h\end{vmatrix}  =   aei+bfg+cdh-ceg-bdi-afh$
